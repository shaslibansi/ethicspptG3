Ethical Implications and Applications

-Focus on rights, justice, and fairness.


Justice means giving each person what they deserve and ensuring laws are applied fairly. It includes distributive justice (fair resource sharing), procedural justice (fair processes), and retributive justice (fair punishment). True justice promotes peace and trust, as seen when rich and poor face the same consequences for the same offense (Johnson & Wales University, 2023).

Fairness, closely linked to justice, focuses on impartiality and respect in daily life. It means understanding people’s unique situations rather than treating everyone identically. Beyond Intractability (2022) notes that fairness requires consistency and transparency, while Ethical Systems (2023) highlights that perceived fairness builds trust. For example, fair pay and promotion in workplaces or teachers giving extra help to struggling students show fairness in action.
