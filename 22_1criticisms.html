Moral importance of principle and dignity
  • Duty over consequences: Moral actions are judged by the motive(or Duty) behind them, not by the outcome they produce.

  • The Categorical Imperative l: A universal moral command that is not conditional on one's desire or Goal.

  • Treating people as ends: Whether in your own person or in the person of any other never as merely as means to an end,But always same time as an end.

  • Intrinsic Worth : This principle recognizes that every human has inherent dignity and value and should be respected for their own sake, not simply used to advance another's agenda.
